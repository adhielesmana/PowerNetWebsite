About PowerNet

Licensed ISP and ICT systems integrator serving Liberia since 2014.

Established in 2014, PowerNet has grown from a broadband-focused provider into one of Liberia's leading licensed ISPs and end-to-end ICT solutions integrators. The company now supports organizations with internet connectivity, enterprise networking, firewall protection, network management, physical security, voice systems and managed infrastructure services.

Its seven operating divisions cover satellite and internet services, security and surveillance, voice and collaboration, networking and integration, enterprise ICT, public address systems and nationwide support services. PowerNet combines local execution capability with global vendor backing to deliver reliable, cost-effective and future-ready systems.

PowerNet works directly with leading global manufacturers including Starlink, Airlive, Tarana, Mimosa, Matrix, SonicWall, Ajax and MikroTik. That direct vendor alignment helps us deliver authentic solutions, competitive pricing, reliable spare-parts availability and vendor-backed technical support that protects long-term continuity for every deployment.

PowerNet is also a member of the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), helping us align with international standards, contribute practical insight on Liberia's infrastructure, and support wider access to internet and technology for people and communities across Liberia.

Connectivity & Infrastructure

Redundant international capacity, resilient access design and business-grade last-mile delivery.

PowerNet maintains strong international and regional connectivity through 4G capacity via Lisbon, 1G via Paris and a terrestrial route to Abidjan, all delivered through CCL. The network is backed by IP Transit from Spain, a primary POP in Lisbon, an additional POP at CCL and multiple redundant paths into Europe through the ACE submarine cable system.

Our Network Operations Center is connected through dedicated CSquared fiber with a redundant wireless point-to-point link. Tarana and Mimosa platforms operating in the 5 and 6 GHz bands support both point-to-point and point-to-multipoint deployments, with throughput of more than 1 Gbps per link.

PowerNet is engineered for 99.9% uptime, with latency to Europe not exceeding 120 ms. Towers are interconnected in an X pattern so traffic reroutes automatically in the event of equipment or tower failure. Customer links are provisioned from your premises to one of our towers or direct fiber, then into our 12th Street, Sinkor backbone, using a Tarana or Mimosa CPE, router and bandwidth management platform for stable performance and remote monitoring.
